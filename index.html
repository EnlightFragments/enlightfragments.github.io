  • About Human

    We aspire to perfection, which we have drawn for ourselves. We are a perfect idea, privileged to think that we are able to look inside ourselves from the outside perspective. We draw every path we can imagine. We get sucked into life-long competition, from start to finish. That’s when we lose sight of who we are, and the importance of our will. We choose to create our egos and identify with them. Then we inflate it as high as we can.

    This makes it much harder to harness our will. It makes us ask “What should I do to make progress?” instead of “What do I want?”.

    It becomes much clearer when you understand your own intentions, covert motives, that’s when you truly start to think.

    Take a look at what you see every day and ask yourself: What is it all about? What intention does it serve? Does it conform with your intentions? Then life won’t be so boring.

  • Mechanics

    We think via mechanics, but never notice it.

    Mechanics of ownership: “every thing has a connection to someone, who we call owner. Owner has right for making decisions, on how to use object of ownership.”

    Mechanics of questions: “question is a statement which implies that response should be given in a form that clarifies subject of question.”

    Mechanics of problems: “problem is and entity which prevents us from living desirable life. Every problem implies that it has some kind of solution, which is sequence of actions in order to resolve the problem.”

    …

    Being ridiculously obvious, these mechanics keep getting out of notice. But when declared explicitly, doubt appears, if base of how human thinks can be something more than just adaptation to survive.

    Lets contunue.

    Mechanics of adaptation: “adaptation is a process of keeping organism from being degraded in changing conditions in order to sustain ability to show signs of life. This implies that environment and individual are separate entities, and individual is the one who adapts to changing environment.”

    …

    These are entire mechanics of thinking process. That’s what gets revealed when you declare these mechanics explicitly and question their reasonability.

    …
