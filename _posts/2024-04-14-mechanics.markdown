We are thinking in mechanics, but never notice it.

Mechanics of ownership: "every thing has a connection to someone, who we call owner.
Owner has right for making decisions, on how to use object of ownership."

Mechanics of questions: "question is a statement which implies that response should be given
in a form that clarifies subject of question."

Mechanics of problems: "problem is and entity which prevents us from living desirable life.
Every problem implies that it has some kind of solution, which is sequence of actions
in order to resolve the problem."

...

Being ridiculously obvious, these mechanics keep getting out of notice.
But when declared explicitly, doubt appears, if base of how human thinks can be
something more than just adaptation to survive.

Lets contunue.

Mechanics of adaptation: "adaptation is a process of keeping organism from being degraded
in changing conditions in order to sustain ability to show signs of life.
This implies that environment and individual are separate entities, and individual
is the one who adapts to changing environment."

...

These are entire mechanics of thinking process. That's what gets revealed when you
declare these mechanics explicitly and question their reasonability.

...

"There is self and everything else, which is the world. These are separate entities."

"There is view, which is representation of entity for individual."

"There is body and there is soul. Body is mortal. Soul is immortal. Soul resides somewhere in your head."

"There is theory. It gets shaped in books, which can be read. This is how theory meterializes by residing in minds."

"There is act of creation. Raw material gets shaped into something it never was before.
Of course there is master, because it won't get shaped by itself."

"There is organism. It consists of lots of different parts which work together."

Why do all these make sense?
